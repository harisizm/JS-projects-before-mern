Classic Beef Burger

Description

This classic beef burger recipe creates the perfect juicy, flavorful burger that's better than any restaurant. Made with fresh ground beef and seasoned to perfection, these burgers are grilled to achieve the ideal balance of crispy exterior and tender, juicy interior. Whether you're hosting a barbecue or just want a satisfying meal, this recipe will become your go-to for the ultimate burger experience.

Ingredients

For the Burger Patties:

  • 1.5 lbs ground beef (80/20 blend for best flavor)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil

For Assembly:

  • 4 hamburger buns
  • 4 slices of cheese (cheddar or American)
  • 1 large tomato, sliced
  • 1 red onion, sliced
  • 4 lettuce leaves
  • 4 pickle slices
  • Mayonnaise
  • Ketchup
  • Mustard

Steps

  1. Remove ground beef from refrigerator 30 minutes before cooking to bring to room temperature.
  2. In a large bowl, gently combine ground beef, salt, pepper, garlic powder, onion powder, and Worcestershire sauce.
  3. Divide the mixture into 4 equal portions and gently form into patties slightly larger than your buns (they'll shrink while cooking).
  4. Create a small indentation in the center of each patty with your thumb to prevent puffing.
  5. Preheat your grill or skillet to medium-high heat. Brush with olive oil.
  6. Cook patties for 3-4 minutes on the first side without pressing down.
  7. Flip burgers and cook for another 3-4 minutes for medium doneness.
  8. If adding cheese, place on patties during the last minute of cooking.
  9. Meanwhile, lightly toast the burger buns on the grill or in a toaster.
  10. Let patties rest for 2-3 minutes after cooking.
  11. Spread mayo, ketchup, and mustard on the bottom bun.
  12. Layer with lettuce, tomato, burger patty with cheese, onion, and pickles.
  13. Top with the other half of the bun and serve immediately.

Tips for the Perfect Burger

  • Don't overmix the meat - this can make burgers tough
  • Never press down on burgers while cooking - it squeezes out the juices
  • Use a meat thermometer: 160°F for well-done, 155°F for medium
  • Let the meat rest after cooking to redistribute juices
  • Toast the buns for better texture and to prevent sogginess
  • Serve with crispy fries and your favorite sides

Variations

  • Bacon Burger: Add 2-3 strips of crispy bacon
  • Mushroom Swiss: Top with sautéed mushrooms and Swiss cheese
  • BBQ Burger: Add BBQ sauce and onion rings
  • Spicy Burger: Add jalapeños and pepper jack cheese
